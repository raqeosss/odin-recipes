Brazilian Pesto

Description

The pesto sauce is strong, nutty, refreshing, a little spicy, easy to make and addictive. It's divine for pasta, pizza, toast, grilled chicken and fish, but pour it on whatever your heart tells you to and I guarantee it'll taste amazing.

Today we are bringing you this classic of the Genovese Cuisine with a dash of the Brazilian gingado. So prep your kitchen counter for some action and stick around to quickly learn how to rock — or rather, samba — this sauce!

Info

Will be ready in: 10 minutes

Utensils required: Food Processor

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of basil
  • ½ cup of toasted unsalted cashew nuts
  • 1 cup of grated parmesan
  • 3~4 garlic cloves
  • 1½ cups of olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper

Steps

  1. Rinse the basil leaves and dry them up. Peel the garlic cloves.
  2. Using a food processor, mix the olive oil and the nuts first, then add the garlic and the cheese. Lastly, add the basil leaves and mix until all of the ingredients are nicely ground together.
  3. Add salt and pepper to your liking, mix with a spoon and it is ready!

Conservation

To avoid oxidation, store the sauce in a glass container with a thin layer of olive oil on top and seal it. You can keep it in the fridge for roughly 2 weeks or in the freezer for up to 3 months.

You can also freeze it in an ice tray, take out one or two cubes at a time, heat for 1 minute in the microwave and enjoy it in long-lasting and practical portions.
