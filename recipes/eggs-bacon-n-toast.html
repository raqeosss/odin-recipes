Description

We're pretty sure you must have had Eggs and Bacon at least a couple of times, and we know nothing beats a classic dish, so today you are invited not to reinvent the wheel, but to try to spin it differently, you are invited to play with texture and heat.

Info

Will be ready in: 20 minutes

Utensils required: Pan, Spatula

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 4 strips of bacon
  • 2 slices of bread
  • Salt
  • Odin's Brazilian Pesto (optional)

Steps

  1. Fry the bacon strips at low heat on a pan. You can use a little bit of butter or olive oil for this step if you think your pan will stick, but overall — specially if you are using non-stick pans or stainless steel pans — it is not needed.
  2. Meanwhile, crack the eggs in a cup, salt them and mix them with a fork or a whisk. Keep an eye on the bacons trips, not letting them stick or burn, and flipping them once in a while. When they are nicely browned and caramelized, put them in a plate. Do not remove the bacon's oil from the pan, as it'll be needed for the toasts and eggs.
  3. With the pan on medium heat, toast both sides of the bread slices until they are a bit crisp on the outside. Put them aside on a plate along the bacon.
  4. Lastly, it's time for the eggs. If you still have a bit of that bacon oil, you can go ahead and pour the salted eggs into the pan on low heat. If your pan is already dry, you can use a little bit of butter or olive oil instead.
  5. For this step, it's mandatory that you pay close attention to the eggs. You should keep mixing them around the pan with a spatula or a spoon non-stop, and avoid the egg to cook too much or stick to the pan. It will cook slowly and when they start losing a bit of the egg's wet "shine", you should not cook them further. The result should be shiny and creamy, but not raw at all.
  6. Assemble it all together, and there you have it! If you still want to spice things up a little, try our Brazilian Pesto as a spread for the toasts.
